Phase 1: Dynamic Warm-up (15-20 min)

This phase gradually prepares your body for intense work. Complete all steps.

  • General Cardio (5 min): Light jog, jumping jacks, or cycling.
  • Dynamic Stretches & Mobility (5-7 min): Arm circles, shoulder rolls, wrist circles, and finger extensions.
  • Light Pinch Lifts (5-8 min): Perform light, low-intensity pinch lifts. This includes 2-3 ramp-up sets, gradually increasing weight to 80% of your estimated working weight.

Phase 2: Determine Your Training Weight (10s Pinch Test)

Find a weight that allows you to maintain a **10-second pinch hold** with near-maximal effort. If you can easily hold it for 15+ seconds, the weight is too light. Rest 2-5 minutes between test attempts.

Phase 3: Pinch Block Lifts

This protocol focuses on sustained, maximal isometric pinch strength.

  • Sets per Arm: Perform 5 sets for each arm. (Total 5 cycles of Right Arm lift, Left Arm lift).
  • Reps per Set: Each arm performs 1 single lift per set.
  • Hold Duration: 10 seconds ON.
  • Intra-set Rest: 30 seconds OFF between alternating arms.
  • Rest Between Cycles: Take a 3-minute rest AFTER both hands have completed their lifts within a cycle.

Phase 4: Cool-down (5-10 min)

Helps transition your body to a state of recovery.

  • General Shaking: Shake out hands and arms to release tension.
  • Static Stretches (20-30s each): Gently stretch forearm flexors and extensors, wrists, and fingers.
